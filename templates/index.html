Avanew
Canada's Leading SFR Asset Manager

Avanew has pioneered the Single Family Rental asset class in Canada by assembling the largest portfolio of accessible, institutional-quality rental homes in Ontario. We acquire existing, underdeveloped or underinvested residential real estate assets and complete significant renovations to enhance the livability, life-safety, convenience, energy efficiency and aesthetic of what's available to Canadians.

Our Impact

Since the inception of the Avanew Homes Ukrainian Aid Program ("AHUAP") in 2022, we have welcomed 20 families into our Fund II properties. Nearly all of the newcomers who are of working age have secured employment, and all of the children are enrolled in both school and extracurricular activities. The families have a strong sense of community; with the assistance of our wonderful partners at the Canadian Ukrainian Immigrant Aid Society (CUIAS) and the local Ukrainian communities in our placement cities, they have swiftly adapted to life in Ontario.

Strategy

Development

The purchase and redevelopment underutilized properties is a strategic move that can significantly uplift the standard of living and enhance community value. As rundown houses are reinvigorated, neighborhoods experience transformation with an increase in safety standards and property values. By providing better-quality housing, this approach ensures residents have access to comfortable, modern living conditions, thereby raising the overall standard of living and creating a positive growth cycle.

Build to Rent

The housing market in Ontario has been marked by escalating home prices, putting the prospect of home ownership out of reach for many individuals and families. Yet, the need for secure, quality housing remains paramount. Building homes for rentals offers a viable solution, ensuring that even those who can't afford to buy a home have access to decent, affordable housing. This not only increases the overall housing stock but also helps maintain a balanced, diverse community where both homeownership and renting are accessible options for residents of all income levels. It provides a safety net for those affected by the financial constraints of the current market, leading to more stable, sustainable living conditions across the province.

Stabilization

The stability of housing through rent regulation and consistent tenant occupancy is a crucial pillar for enhancing tenants' quality of life and security. This strategy ensures fair and predictable housing costs, making it easier for renters to budget effectively and lessen the risk of displacement due to abrupt rent increases. When properties are consistently occupied, they are better maintained, creating healthier, safer living conditions for tenants. Moreover, long-term tenancies foster a greater sense of community among residents, enhancing their social well-being and attachment to the neighborhood.

Milestone History

  • March 2020

    Pilot Project

    Our 'Pilot Program' marked the acquisition of 10 properties amounting to 20 units.

  • March 2021

    Fund I

    Solidification of "Fund I" by successfully raising $30M in equity. This was invested into the aquisition 100 homes (200 units) across Ontario. Additionally "Fund I" attracted around $70M in acquisition financing, enabling further aquisitions and renovation.

  • December 2021

    Fund I Financing

    $80M was secured in term financing, to refinance the acquisition debt of "Fund I".

  • February 2022

    Fund II

    $30M in equity was raised to facilitate the acquisition of a portfolio of 228 Single Family Residential (SFR) properties and 311 units across Ontario, collectively identified as "Fund II" and valued at $80M

  • July 2022

    Ukrainian Housing

    In conjunction with the launch of "Fund II", a Ukrainian Aid Program was introduced, offering rent-free housing options to Ukrainian families displaced to Canada. This altruistic initiative further exemplifies a commitment to social responsibility within the broader investment strategy.

  • Fall 2023

    Fund III

    Raise approximately $300M in equity during 2022/2023. This will be known as "Fund III", which targets the acquisition of a diverse mix of both developmental and stabilized Single Family Residential (SFR) assets across Canada.
